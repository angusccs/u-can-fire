Stage 2 — Eliminate High-Interest Debt

High-interest debt (often above 8%–20%) grows faster than almost any safe investment. Paying it off early is one of the most reliable ways to improve your financial health. This isn’t about shame — millions of Canadians carry debt — it’s about creating a clean slate so your money starts working for you instead of against you.

During this stage, you’ll organize your debts, choose a repayment method (snowball or avalanche), and create a simple plan you can follow consistently. Even small weekly or bi-weekly payments add up quickly when interest stops compounding against you.

This stage lays the foundation for everything that comes next. Once high-interest debt is gone, your cash flow frees up dramatically, your stress decreases, and you’re ready to build a proper safety buffer.

Checklist

  • List all debts and interest rates
  • Choose “avalanche” (math) or “snowball” (motivation)
  • Reduce discretionary spending temporarily
  • Pause investing until debt >8% is cleared
