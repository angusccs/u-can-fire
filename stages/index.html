Stages — A quick overview

The site uses a simple staged framework to help you focus on the most important financial step for your situation. Click a stage to read the short guide for that level.

Stage list

  1. Stage 1 — Stabilize Your Base (Mini Emergency Fund)
    Build a $1,000–$2,000 starter emergency fund and begin tracking basic expenses.
    Read Stage 1 →
  2. Stage 2 — Eliminate High-Interest Debt
    Prioritise paying off credit cards and other high-rate debt to reduce interest and regain cash flow.
    Read Stage 2 →
  3. Stage 3 — Build a 3–6 Month Safety Buffer
    Save several months of living expenses to protect against larger shocks and give you time to plan.
    Read Stage 3 →
  4. Stage 4 — Understand TFSA vs RRSP
    Learn which registered account fits your tax situation and long-term goals.
    Read Stage 4 →
  5. Stage 5 — Start Investing (Index Funds & ETFs)
    Begin regular investing into low-cost, diversified ETFs or index funds.
    Read Stage 5 →
  6. Stage 6 — Long-Term Freedom & FI Basics
    Refine long-term goals like FI, Coast FI or retirement and maintain consistent saving and investing habits.
    Read Stage 6 →
