Stage 3 — Build a 3–6 Month Safety Buffer

A full emergency fund protects you from job loss, medical expenses, or unexpected life events. Unlike the starter fund in Stage 1, this buffer is meant to cover your essential living expenses for several months, giving you real peace of mind and financial stability.

You don’t have to save it all at once. Many people build this buffer slowly over time — even a few hundred dollars per month makes steady progress. The purpose is not to chase returns, but to create security and reduce anxiety.

Once this fund is complete, you can confidently move on to investing and optimizing without worrying that one setback will undo your progress. This stage is often the turning point where finances stop feeling chaotic and begin to feel manageable.

Checklist

  • Calculate 3–6 months of essential expenses
  • Save into a high-interest savings or cash account
  • Avoid investing this fund — safety first
  • Review annually as life changes
