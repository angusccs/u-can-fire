Stage 4 — Understand TFSA vs RRSP

Knowing the difference between a TFSA and an RRSP is one of the most important parts of Canadian personal finance. Both accounts have tax advantages, but they work differently and are better suited for different incomes and goals. Once you understand the basics, you can make smarter decisions about where to put your money.

A general rule of thumb is: use a TFSA if you’re in a lower to mid-range income, and an RRSP if you’re higher-income or planning long-term retirement contributions. But there are exceptions, and part of this stage is learning what fits your situation.

You don’t need to memorize every rule. You only need enough clarity to know which account aligns with your tax bracket, income stability, and future plans. After this stage, investing becomes much more intuitive.

Checklist

  • Learn the basic differences between TFSA and RRSP
  • Know your tax bracket (approximate)
  • Decide which account to prioritize
  • Open the account(s) if needed
