Resources

Books

These are beginner-friendly, timeless, and apply well to Canadians:

  • The Psychology of Money — Morgan Housel
    A short, meaningful book on how behaviour shapes financial outcomes.
  • If You Can — William Bernstein
    A simple, free guide that forms a strong foundation.
  • The Little Book of Common Sense Investing — John Bogle
    A classic introduction to index funds and why they work.
  • A Random Walk Down Wall Street — Burton Malkiel
    Explains market behaviour and why low-cost investing wins.
  • The Simple Path to Wealth — JL Collins
    A straightforward guide to long-term investing and financial independence.
  • Millionaire Teacher — Andrew Hallam (Canadian author)
    A practical look at investing from a Canadian perspective.
  • Wealthing Like Rabbits — Robert Brown (Canadian)
    A fun, light introduction to Canadian personal finance basics.
