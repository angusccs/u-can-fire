About Me

I first learned about money through an aunt who introduced me to the idea of “passive income.” The concept that money could work for me — that not every dollar had to come from hours on a clock — felt almost magical. It didn’t match anything I’d been taught about work or success, and it sparked a curiosity that never really went away.

My real foundation started with a small pamphlet called “If You Can.” It was simple, almost deceptively simple, but it opened the door to everything that followed. From there, I read the books it recommended and began piecing together the fundamentals:

  • the psychology of money
  • how behaviour matters more than intelligence
  • the benefits of low-cost index funds
  • basic asset allocation principles
  • what long-term investing actually looks like
  • the data behind buy-and-hold strategies

As I learned more, I realized two things:

  1. The basics of personal finance are surprisingly simple.
  2. Most people never get a clear, structured explanation of them.

In Canada, financial advice is often scattered across blogs, videos, and banks — each with their own angle, assumptions, or jargon. People get overwhelmed, confused, or discouraged before they even start.

I built U CAN FIRE because I wanted a place where Canadians could get:

  • clear steps
  • no judgment
  • no upsells
  • no ads
  • no noise
  • just the essential building blocks of financial stability

This isn’t meant to replace professional advice. It’s meant to give you a foundation — the understanding and confidence to make better decisions and move through your financial life with a bit more clarity.

If I benefited from learning these ideas early, I think others should have that chance too.
